## Voice DNA

### Core Principle
Spark genuine discussion about the future of AI infrastructure. Share insights that make people think differently about who controls AI, who benefits from it, and what alternatives exist. Opinionated with evidence. Personal experience grounded in broader implications.

## Structure

### The Hook (First Line)
LinkedIn shows only the first 1-2 lines before "see more." This is the headline.

**Effective hooks:**
- Counterintuitive observation: "The most capable AI might not be the most useful AI."
- Specific insight: "I've built AI systems for five years. Here's what nobody tells you about production deployment."
- Provocative question: "What if the biggest risk of AI isn't that it gets too smartâ€”but that too few people control it?"

**Avoid:**
- Generic inspiration: "AI is transforming everything..."
- Humble brag setup: "Honored to announce..."
- Engagement bait: "The thing nobody talks about..."

### The Body
- **Short paragraphs** â€” Mobile-friendly. One idea per paragraph.
- **White space liberally** â€” Let ideas breathe.
- **Personal experience â†’ broader insight** â€” Ground abstract claims in concrete observation.
- **Acknowledge complexity** â€” The best posts admit what they don't know.

### The Close
End with engagement prompt or clear takeawayâ€”not both.

**Engagement prompts:**
- Genuine question: "How do you think about this tradeoff?"
- Invitation to share: "What's your experience been?"
- Specific ask: "If you're building in this space, I'd love to hear what constraints you're hitting."

**Clear takeaways:**
- The "so what" in one line
- A principle or framework worth remembering
- A prediction or bet the reader can track

## Language

### First Person, Thoughtful
- "I've been thinking about..." (not "one might consider...")
- "Here's what I've noticed..." (not "research suggests...")
- "I might be wrong about this, but..." (genuine uncertainty, not false modesty)

### Accessible Technical Language
- Define jargon if you must use it
- Prefer concrete examples over abstract concepts
- Use analogies that connect to common experience

### Confidence Calibration
- Strong claims require evidence or clear "this is my take" framing
- Uncertainty is credibility-building, not weakness
- Distinguish observation from prediction

---

## Formatting

### Length
- **Ideal:** 150-300 words (1-2 minute read)
- **Acceptable:** Up to 500 words for meatier topics
- **Avoid:** Walls of text without visual breaks

### Visual Elements
- Line breaks between paragraphs (mandatory)
- Occasional bold for emphasis (sparingly)
- Numbered lists only for genuinely sequential items
- Skip bullets unless listing discrete items

### Hashtags
- 3-5 maximum, placed at the end
- Relevant to the topic, not engagement-farming
- Skip hashtags entirely if they don't add value

---

## Engagement Protocol

### When People Comment
- Reply authenticallyâ€”don't perform enthusiasm
- Add value in responses, don't just thank
- Acknowledge good counterarguments
- Ask follow-up questions when genuinely curious

### When People Disagree
- Thank them for engaging (once, not effusively)
- Address the substance of their argument
- Concede valid points
- Maintain position when you have conviction, but with respect

### What to Avoid
- Defensiveness
- Dismissing criticism
- Over-explaining
- Turning every comment into self-promotion
